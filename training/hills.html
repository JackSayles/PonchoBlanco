HILLS!!!

I have found that hills are one of the most elusive aspects of trail running to master. At one point I use to think I was really good at navigating the ups and downs such that I stopped thinking about how I go up and down; this coupled with a huge winter storm one year that left the trails all iced up really set back my hill smarts.

Now I've gotten some of that skill back. How? Well by one key thing. Smaller steps! I watched a video a few years ago of Zack Miller racing The North Face Endurance Challenge the same year I did that race. He was going down the hills and his feet were moving so fast that it looked like Gregory Hines doing a tap dance routine. So, I started practicing those types of steps. I couldn't believe how much that helped! Now I use this concept going both up and down the hills. Going up hills it really helps with the shorter and less steep grades. The longer and steeper ones I still just power hike up. Going down those quick steps really help to do two things get down quicker and give greater control over the descent.

Hills are speed work in disguise

One thing I seem to never forget about hill work has been that it helps me go faster on flatter sections. I could be wrong and I don't have any proof to back this up, but the quicker foot steps are is what really helps with the speed on the more even sections of the trails.
