Adventure Runs

What is an adventure run? It is a run that is either a training run or a fun run, for me many times they are both. It is also not where you normally train. It generally takes me about an hour and a half or longer to drive to the spot where I'm going to run for it to fall in this catagory as well. Plus I'm either carrying everything I'm going to need or I'm planning my own aid stations, like running back to the car to get more food and water. A lot of times there is some sort of awe associated with where the run will take place. Example Frozen Head is where The Barkley Marathons and the Smoky Mountains has the Appalachian Trail, at this point this trail will simply be called the 'AT'. Some of the other locations for me have beautiful water falls or Daniel Boone moved through the area often or John Muir blazed a trail or used a trail through the region.

The style of this section will be more a long the idea of a blog. I will share with you what I experianced while on the trail.

So the name 'Poncho Blanco', how did it come to be? Well I was making my first SCAR attempt. Several hikers had asked me what my trail name was, which I didn't have one at the time. About 4ish hours into the run it started to rain and thunder, so I pulled out my $5 white poncho that I had picked up that morning. Apparently I had startled several folks with my poncho. Later after the run was done and I was with The Dirtbag Trail Club telling stories of the adventures had everyone got a good laugh out of the poncho stories. With that as a back drop and the group trying to come up with a trail name for me someone blurst out how 'bout 'Poncho Blanco'? All around die laughing so the name stuck.

Poncho Blanco
